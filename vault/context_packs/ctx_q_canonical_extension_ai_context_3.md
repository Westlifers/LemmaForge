Given a distributive lattice L, its canonical extension $L^δ$ is the downset lattice of the poset of prime filters of L, ordered by inclusion.

### 3. Tentative definition of Q-canonical extension

- ID: `q_canonical_extension_definition`
- Type: Definition
- Status: draft
- Origin: mixed
- Exactness: original
- Selection reason: The central tentative definition: a complete separated Q-category with a fully faithful embedding satisfying density and compactness.

A \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extension of \(\mathcal A\) is a pair \((\mathcal A^\delta,\eta)\), where \(\mathcal A^\delta\) is a complete separated \(\mathcal Q\)-category and \(\eta:\mathcal A\to\mathcal A^\delta\) is a fully faithful \(\mathcal Q\)-functor. It satisfies density, meaning every object of \(\mathcal A^\delta\) can be expressed as both a weighted colimit and a weighted limit of objects from \(\mathcal A\), and compactness, meaning that whenever a formal inequality between such weighted joins and meets holds in \(\mathcal A^\delta\), it is already witnessed by a finite subconfiguration inside \(\mathcal A\).

### 5. Possible Phi-canonical refinement of compactness

- ID: `possible_phi_canonical_extension_refinement`
- Type: Remark
- Status: draft

- Selection reason: Records a possible refinement of the definition by restricting compactness to a class of weights Phi, directly relevant to interpreting the tentative definition.

The finite-witness compactness condition may be too strong for general quantaloids. A weaker version might require compactness only for weights generated by representables, or for weights belonging to a chosen class \(\Phi\). In that case one should speak of a \(\Phi\)-canonical extension rather than an unrestricted \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extension.
