### 2. Tentative definition of Q-canonical extension

- ID: `q_canonical_extension_definition`
- Type: Definition
- Status: draft
- Origin: mixed
- Exactness: original
- Selection reason: Core tentative definition of Q-canonical extension, including completeness, full faithfulness, density, and compactness assumptions.

A \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extension of \(\mathcal A\) is a pair \((\mathcal A^\delta,\eta)\), where \(\mathcal A^\delta\) is a complete separated \(\mathcal Q\)-category and \(\eta:\mathcal A\to\mathcal A^\delta\) is a fully faithful \(\mathcal Q\)-functor. It satisfies density, meaning every object of \(\mathcal A^\delta\) can be expressed as both a weighted colimit and a weighted limit of objects from \(\mathcal A\), and compactness, meaning that whenever a formal inequality between such weighted joins and meets holds in \(\mathcal A^\delta\), it is already witnessed by a finite subconfiguration inside \(\mathcal A\).

### 4. Possible Phi-canonical refinement of compactness

- ID: `possible_phi_canonical_extension_refinement`
- Type: Remark
- Status: draft
- Origin: mixed
- Exactness: original
- Selection reason: Introduces the Phi-restricted version likely needed for the alternative objective about Phi-weighted limits.

The finite-witness compactness condition may be too strong for general quantaloids. A weaker version might require compactness only for weights generated by representables, or for weights belonging to a chosen class \(\Phi\). In that case one should speak of a \(\Phi\)-canonical extension rather than an unrestricted \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extension.

### 5. Reflection of existing weighted limits and colimits

- ID: `reflection_of_existing_weighted_limits_colimits`
- Type: Proposition
- Status: draft
- Origin: mixed
- Exactness: original
- Selection reason: Most direct existing claim about how eta interacts with weighted limits and colimits already present in A.

If \((\mathcal A^\delta,\eta)\) is a \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extension, then \(\eta\) reflects all existing weighted limits and weighted colimits in \(\mathcal A\). In particular, if a join or meet already exists in \(\mathcal A\), its image under \(\eta\) computes the corresponding join or meet in \(\mathcal A^\delta\).

### 6. Tentative presheaf closure construction

- ID: `closure_inside_presheaves_construction`
- Type: Construction
- Status: draft
- Origin: mixed
- Exactness: original
- Selection reason: Provides a construction strategy: build A^delta by closing Yoneda images under selected weighted limits and colimits.

A possible construction of \(\mathcal A^\delta\) is to take it as the closure of \(Y_{\mathcal A}(\mathcal A)\) inside \(P\mathcal A\) under a specified class of weighted limits and weighted colimits. Under suitable exactness assumptions on \(\mathcal Q\), this construction is expected to produce a complete separated \(\mathcal Q\)-category.

### 7. Conditional initiality of the closure construction

- ID: `initiality_of_closure_canonical_extension`
- Type: Theorem
- Status: draft
- Origin: mixed
- Exactness: original
- Selection reason: May support uniqueness or functorial transfer arguments, but only under explicit exactness and compactness-stability hypotheses.

Assume that \(\mathcal Q\) admits the exactness needed for the closure construction of \(\mathcal A^\delta\), and assume that the compactness condition is stable under the relevant weighted limits and weighted colimits. Then the resulting pair \((\mathcal A^\delta,Y_{\mathcal A})\) is initial among \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extensions of \(\mathcal A\). More precisely, for every \(\mathcal Q\)-canonical extension \((\mathcal B,\eta')\) of \(\mathcal A\), there is a unique complete \(\mathcal Q\)-functor \(F:\mathcal A^\delta\to\mathcal B\) such that \(F\circ Y_{\mathcal A}=\eta'\).
